What is Reclaim Philly?

Reclaim Philly is a vacant property tracking application that aggregates known public data with crowd-sourced information to create a more complete, dynamic database of Philadelphia’s vacant properties.

Philadelphia has a vacant property crisis: A recent study commissioned by the Philadelphia Association of Community Development Corporations (PACDC) and the Philadelphia Redevelopment Authority estimates that there are at least 40,000 vacant properties in the city.

  • These blighted properties costs the city over $20 million a year to maintain
  • Property values across the City are reduced by at least $3.6 billion as a result of this blight.
  • 25% or about 10,000 of these properties are owned by a number of public agencies.
  • The other 75% of vacant properties are in private hands, and largely undocumented, with no centralized database.

Reclaim Philly is a platform that will use web and mobile applications to aggregate existing publicly accessible data alongside citizen and community organization reported information (data and photos) about vacant properties in their neighborhoods. Reclaim Philly will be a centralized database that can not only map the instances of vacancy, but also pull from the other public data sets to create a complete profile of Philly's vacant land - ownership, parcel size, liens, crime, zoning, taxes, and more.

Reclaim Philly will also be a platform for people to connect and organize around vacant property in their community by providing information on how to access these vacant properties. This may be through Philadelphia’s system for vacant property disposition, petitioning for foreclosure for tax delinquency, or through new tools. We will partner with other groups engaged in short-term and long-term plans for reuse to further engage people about efforts to revitalize their neighborhoods and how they can help while also providing a resource to the media and reporters to see how vacancy and blight is affecting our City. Individuals will not only find a conduit for their desire to improve their neighborhoods but entire communities will now be able to plan around how these properties are reused.

While we are targeting all Philadelphians, we will provide local neighborhood groups with incentives to participate: the ability to define their area and download our data sets in map-ready forms (via standard or well documented formats/APIs). We hope that this will provide additional motivation to participate in areas that have limited access to mobile phones with app capability. We also plan to work to integrate it and make it compatible with key city systems to increase the value this data and tracking can provide to City government.

We know the time is right for Reclaim Philly because our City is experiencing a renewed spirit of community investment and concern for our vacant land. Reclaim Philly will be an innovative tool available to citizens, community groups, city government, and the press to help fully understand the scope of our City's vacant property problem for the first time and turn the vacant parcels from one our communities’ biggest problems into one our biggest assets.

Who is working on this?

The project grew out of a Code for America Hackathon in 2012 and is currently a work in progress between the Philadelphia Association of Community Development Corporations (PACDC) and volunteer programming and design professionals. Following Code for America's philosophy, we're developing the software as open source and hope to be able to enable other cities to take or build on our successful work.
